# Instructor (Content Creator) Workflow

This guide walks through the instructor experience on Sycom LMS, from account creation to editing courses, managing curriculum, and adding interactive quizzes to video lessons.

---

## 1. Create Your Account

1. Navigate to the **Sign Up** page (`/sign-up`).
2. Fill in your details:
   - **First name** and **Last name**
   - **Email address** (use your organization email)
   - **Password** (minimum 8 characters, must include uppercase, lowercase, and a number)
3. Click **Create account**.
4. You will be redirected to the Sign In page with a confirmation message.

## 2. Verify Your Email

Check your inbox for a verification email from Sycom LMS. Click the verification link to activate your account.

> **Note:** Your account must be verified before you can sign in.

## 3. Get Instructor Access

A platform administrator must assign you the **Content Creator** role and add you as an instructor on the relevant course(s). Contact your admin to:

- Set your platform role to **Content Creator**
- Add you as a **co-instructor** on the course(s) you will manage

Once assigned, the instructor dashboard and course editor become available.

## 4. Sign In

1. Navigate to the **Sign In** page (`/sign-in`).
2. Enter your email and password.
3. Click **Continue**.
4. You will be redirected to the **Instructor Dashboard**.

## 5. Instructor Dashboard Overview

The instructor dashboard provides a snapshot of your teaching workspace:

- **Quick statistics**:
  - Number of **Courses** you manage (published and in progress)
  - **Enrollments** across your visible courses
  - Total **Lessons** available
  - **Blog posts** authored
- **Content pipeline** chart showing draft vs. published items
- **Recently updated courses** for quick access
- **Recent blog activity** tracking your drafts and published posts

## 6. Managing Courses

Click **My Courses** in the sidebar to view all courses assigned to you. From here you can:

- **Search** and **filter** courses by category, difficulty, and status
- Toggle between **Card** and **Table** views
- Click the **edit** (pencil) icon on any course to open the course editor
- Click **+ Create course** to start a new course from scratch

## 7. Editing a Course

The course editor has two tabs:

### Course Info Tab

Edit the course metadata:

- **Thumbnail** image
- **Title** and **Slug** (URL-friendly identifier)
- **Description** (short text for course cards)
- **Summary** (rich text editor for detailed overview shown on the course page)
- **Categories** (e.g., Cybersecurity)
- **Difficulty** level (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, Expert)
- **Publish** toggle to make the course visible to students

Click **Save changes** after editing.

### Curriculum Tab

Manage sections and lessons:

- View all **sections** and their **lessons** in a collapsible tree
- **Drag and drop** to reorder sections and lessons
- Click **+ Add Section** to create a new section
- Click the **+** button on a section to add a new lesson
- Click any lesson title to expand its editor

## 8. Editing a Lesson

When you expand a lesson in the Curriculum tab, a rich text editor (Tiptap) appears with a full formatting toolbar:

- **Text formatting**: Bold, Italic, Underline, Strikethrough, Code
- **Structure**: Headings, Lists, Blockquotes, Tables
- **Alignment**: Left, Center, Right, Justify
- **Media**: Images, Audio, Files, YouTube videos
- **Interactive**: Quiz blocks

## 9. Adding Quizzes to Lessons

To add a quiz to any lesson:

1. Expand the lesson in the **Curriculum** tab.
2. Click at the end of the existing content in the editor.
3. Press **Enter** to create a new line.
4. In the toolbar, click the **quiz button** (circle with question mark icon).
5. A Quiz Block appears with default placeholder content.
6. Edit the quiz:
   - Type your **question** in the question field
   - Edit each **option** text
   - Click the **radio button** next to the correct answer to mark it
   - Toggle between **Single** and **Multiple** answer modes
   - Click **+ Add Option** to add more choices
   - Click the **trash icon** to remove an option
7. Click **Save Lesson** to persist your changes.

### Example Quiz

For a video lesson about the OSI Model, you might add:

- **Question**: "What are the 7 layers of the OSI model from bottom to top?"
- **Option A** (correct): Physical, Data Link, Network, Transport, Session, Presentation, Application
- **Option B**: Application, Presentation, Session, Transport, Network, Data Link, Physical
- **Option C**: Physical, Network, Transport, Data Link, Session, Application, Presentation
- **Option D**: Application, Transport, Network, Physical, Data Link, Session, Presentation

## 10. Co-creators

Click the **Co-creators** button in the top-right corner of the course editor to manage who can collaborate on the course.
